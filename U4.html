🔤 A) One Word, Two Definitions

Many English words have multiple meanings. When AI lacks context, it picks the most common meaning — which may be completely wrong.

🚌💺👥

"coach" = a large

❌ AI thought "coach" = sports trainer 🏀 — the student meant passenger bus 🚌

"Check this email: 'Hi team, a coach (passenger bus) will pick up the 10 VIP guests from the airport at 3pm.'"

💡 Prevention Tip

4.1 Common AI Language Mistakes

In Unit 1, you learned that AI can hallucinate — generating convincing but completely false facts. But AI doesn't just get facts wrong. It also makes language mistakes that can change the meaning of your message entirely.

In this unit, you'll learn to spot four common types of AI language errors:

🔤

A. One Word, Two Definitions

AI picks the wrong meaning of a word

🌐

B. Translation Traps

AI mistranslates technical or trade terms

👤

C. Ambiguous References

AI misses unclear pronouns when proofreading

🌍

D. Cultural Bias

AI defaults to US English norms

⚡ Key Insight

Just like the fake legal cases from Unit 1, AI text can look perfect but still be wrong. You must read, check, and edit every AI output before using it.

🌐 B) Translation Traps

In Section A, you saw how AI picks the wrong meaning of an English word. The same problem gets even trickier across languages — AI translates by the most common meaning, often missing the technical or trade-specific context you need.

🔧 Scenario — Engineering student translating a term
✨ AI Chat
🤖

AI Assistant

Online • Model v4.0

What is "nut" in Chinese?
✨
"Nut" in Chinese is 堅果 (jiānguǒ). It refers to a hard-shelled fruit or seed. 🥜
Type your message…
↑

💭 Student means:

🚗🔧🔩

"nut" = metal
fastener for bolts!

❌ AI gave the food meaning 🥜 — the student needed the engineering meaning 🔩

✏️ If you must use AI, provide full context:

"Translate 'nut' to Chinese. Context: automotive engineering — it is a metal piece with a threaded hole that screws onto a bolt."

💡 Prevention Tip

Provide a complete sentence and name the industry when asking AI to translate. AI is a text predictor, not a dictionary.

👤 C) Ambiguous References

📌 Scenario — Student asks AI to proofread an email
✨ AI Chat
🤖

AI Assistant

Online • Model v4.0

Please proofread this:
"Chris spoke to the supervisor about the broken printer. He said it was too old, so he threw it away."
✨
Your text looks correct! ✅ The grammar and sentence structure are fine. No changes needed.
Type your message…
↑

🔍 But wait…

"He said it was too old"

Chris? or Supervisor?

"he threw it away"

Chris? or Supervisor?

⚠️ Both readings are valid!

⚠️ AI checked the grammar — but missed the meaning problem. The sentences ARE grammatically correct, but the pronouns are ambiguous. AI cannot detect this!

✅ Fixed — replace pronouns with names:

"Chris spoke to the supervisor about the broken printer. The supervisor said it was too old, so Chris threw it away."

💡 Prevention Tip

After AI proofreads, re-read every pronoun yourself. Ask: "Can this pronoun match exactly one person?" If not, replace it with the actual name.

🌍 D) Cultural Bias

AI is heavily trained on American English data. It frequently applies US norms that don't fit Hong Kong.

Common mismatches:

🇺🇸 US English

color

🇭🇰 HK English

colour

🇺🇸 Date format

01/15/2025

🇭🇰 Date format

15/01/2025

🇺🇸 Vocabulary

elevator, fall, check

🇭🇰 Vocabulary

lift, autumn, cheque

🇺🇸 Floors

1st floor = ground

🇭🇰 Floors

G/F = ground level

✏️ Prevention — add a rule to your prompt:

"Proofread my work report. Use British English spelling."

💡 Prevention Tip

Always include "Use British English" in prompts. Check dates, spelling, and vocabulary yourself.

📋 Key Takeaways

🔤

Multiple meanings? Add context or use a clearer synonym so AI picks the right one.

🌐

Technical terms? AI is a text predictor, not a dictionary. Provide full industry context or use a proper dictionary.

👤

Pronouns unclear? AI approves grammar but misses ambiguity. Re-read every pronoun — replace with names if in doubt.

🌍

US vs HK English? Always specify "British English" in prompts. Check spelling and vocabulary yourself.

🧰 Before Using AI Output, Check:

✅ Word meanings correct?
✅ Technical terms accurate?
✅ Pronouns clear?
✅ British English spelling?
