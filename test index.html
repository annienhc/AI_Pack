🔤 A) One Word, Two Definitions

Many English words have multiple meanings. When AI lacks context, it picks the most common meaning — which may be completely wrong.

🏅 Scenario — Sports student arranging airport transfer
✨ AI Chat
🤖

AI Assistant

Online • Model v4.0

Book a coach for the 10 VIP guests arriving from the airport.
✨
Sure! I've found a professional basketball coach available this Saturday. He can run a 2-hour training session for your 10 VIP guests after they arrive from the airport. Want me to confirm the booking? 🏀
Type your message…
↑

❌ AI understood:

Coach = sports trainer

✅ You meant:

Coach = large passenger bus

✏️ Fix: Add context to your prompt

"Book a passenger bus (coach) for the 10 VIP guests arriving from the airport."

💡 More words that confuse AI:

table — furniture or data chart?
bill — banknote or invoice?
plant — flowers or machinery?

💡 Prevention Tip

If a word has multiple meanings, add context or use a clearer synonym: "passenger bus" instead of "coach".

👤 B) Ambiguous References

AI often uses pronouns (he, she, it, they) without making it clear who or what they refer to — especially with two people of the same gender.

📌 Example — Who is "He"?

"Chris spoke to the supervisor about the broken printer. He said it was too old, so he threw it away."

⚠️ Who said it was too old? Who threw it away?

✅ Clearer version:

"Chris spoke to the supervisor about the broken printer. The supervisor said it was too old, so Chris threw it away."

📌 "It" can be confusing too!

"The report was sent to the client. It was updated yesterday."

⚠️ What was updated — the report or the client's file?

✅ Clearer:

"The report was sent to the client. The report was updated yesterday."

💡 How to Fix

When reading AI text, check every pronoun. Replace "he/she/it" with the actual name or noun.

🌐 C) Translation Traps

AI translates words by their most common meaning, often missing the technical or trade-specific context you need.

🔧 Example — Engineering student translating a term
✨ AI Chat
🤖

AI Assistant

Online • Model v4.0

What is "nut" in Chinese?
✨
"Nut" in Chinese is 堅果 (jiānguǒ). It refers to a hard-shelled fruit or seed. 🥜
Type your message…
↑

💭 Student thinks:

🚗🔧🔩

"nut" = metal
fastener for bolts!

❌ AI gave the food meaning 🥜 — the student needed the engineering meaning 🔩

✏️ If you must use AI, provide full context:

"Translate 'nut' to Chinese. Context: automotive engineering — it is a metal piece with a threaded hole that screws onto a bolt."

💡 Prevention Tip

Provide a complete sentence and name the industry when asking AI to translate. AI is a text predictor, not a dictionary.

🌍 D) Cultural Bias

AI is heavily trained on American English data. It frequently applies US norms that don't fit Hong Kong.

Common mismatches:

🇺🇸 US English

color

🇭🇰 HK English

colour

🇺🇸 Date format

01/15/2025

🇭🇰 Date format

15/01/2025

🇺🇸 Vocabulary

elevator, fall, check

🇭🇰 Vocabulary

lift, autumn, cheque

🇺🇸 Floors

1st floor = ground

🇭🇰 Floors

G/F = ground level

✏️ Prevention — add a rule to your prompt:

"Proofread my work report. Use British English spelling."

💡 Prevention Tip

Always include "Use British English" in prompts. Check dates, spelling, and vocabulary yourself.

4.1 Common AI Language Mistakes

In Unit 1, you learned that AI can hallucinate — generating convincing but completely false facts. But AI doesn't just get facts wrong. It also makes language mistakes that can change the meaning of your message entirely.

In this unit, you'll learn to spot four common types of AI language errors:

🔤

A. One Word, Two Definitions

AI picks the wrong meaning of a word

👤

B. Ambiguous References

AI uses unclear pronouns (he, she, it)

🌐

C. Translation Traps

AI mistranslates technical or trade terms

🌍

D. Cultural Bias

AI defaults to US English norms

⚡ Key Insight

Just like the fake legal cases from Unit 1, AI text can look perfect but still be wrong. You must read, check, and edit every AI output before using it.

📋 Key Takeaways

1️⃣

Words with multiple meanings need context

Add synonyms or descriptions to prevent AI from picking the wrong definition.

2️⃣

Always check pronouns in AI text

Replace ambiguous he/she/it with actual names or nouns before using AI text.

3️⃣

AI is not a dictionary

Use proper dictionaries for technical terms. If using AI, provide full industry context.

4️⃣

AI defaults to American English

Always specify "British English" in prompts and double-check spelling, dates, and vocabulary.

🧰 Language Check Checklist

Before you use AI output, ask:
✅ Are all word meanings correct for this context?
✅ Are all pronouns clear?
✅ Are technical terms translated correctly?
✅ Is the spelling & format British / HK English?
