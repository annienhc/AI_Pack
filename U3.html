3.2 Why Does AI Get It Wrong?

AI generates text by predicting the most likely words — but it doesn't truly understand what you need for your job.

If your prompt is unclear, AI will guess the purpose — and it often guesses wrong. The result may be well-written but completely useless for your actual workplace task.

3.3 Three Common Mismatches

When AI misses the purpose, it usually falls into one of these categories:

📄

A. Wrong Text Type

AI writes the wrong format for the situation

🎨

B. Wrong Tone & Style

AI uses the wrong level of formality

⚙️

C. Wrong Language Function

AI uses the wrong function for the task

📋 Instruction

📖 Narrative

🖼 Description

🤝 Offer

💡 Suggestion

🙏 Request

⚠️ Keywords alone aren't enough. Typing just "contacting the supplier" into AI gives it only the topic, not the function. The same keywords can produce completely different sentences depending on what the text needs to do.

3.1 Think About It

Two admin trainees need a new printer for their department. They both typed the same vague prompt into AI:

Both trainees typed:

"Write about buying a new printer for the office"

🤖
AI Chat
HP vs Canon: The Ultimate 2025 Printer Showdown!
Looking for the perfect office printer? Here's our expert comparison…
✗

❌ Product Comparison Blog

🤖
AI Chat
10 Things to Know Before Buying a Printer
1. Laser vs inkjet — which is right?
2. Check the cost per page…
✗

❌ Buying Tips Article

🤔

What did both trainees actually need?

They needed a purchase request email to their manager — asking for budget approval to buy a new printer. Neither a product review nor a buying guide would help! Because the prompt was vague, AI guessed the purpose and got it wrong both times.

⚡ Key Insight

A vague prompt leads to unpredictable results. Always specify the purpose, text type, and audience in your AI prompt!

📄 A. Text Type

A text type is the format of writing. Different tasks need different text types. Let's see what happens when you don't specify one:

🏨 Hotel Receptionist — wants guests to know about breakfast

Prompt:

"Write something about our breakfast buffet for guests"

🤖
AI Chat
"Breakfast Buffet Review ★★★★☆ — The scrambled eggs were fluffy, but the coffee was lukewarm. The pastry selection was decent. Overall, 4 stars."
✗

❌ AI wrote a food review — not useful for hotel guests!

Better Prompt:

"Write a guest information card about our breakfast buffet. Include time, location, and menu."

🤖
AI Chat
"🍳 Breakfast Buffet — Daily, 7:00–10:00am, Ground Floor Restaurant. Choose from Western breakfast, Asian congee, fresh pastries, and more. Enjoy!"
✓

✅ Saying "guest information card" told AI the exact text type needed.

Common workplace text types:

Email Report Memo Letter Notice Leaflet Guide Form Checklist

💡 Tip

Never say "Write about X." Always say "Write a memo / email / guide / notice about X."

🎨 B. Tone & Style

Tone and style refer to how the text sounds. The same information can be written very differently depending on the situation.

📌 Example — Reply about return policy

❌ Too Casual

"yo just bring it back whenever lol we don't really care about receipts 😂"

✅ Just Right

"You may return items within 14 days with the original receipt for a full refund."

Two questions to always ask:

1

Formality

Is it too formal or too casual for this situation?

2

Audience

Does it match who will read it? (clients / colleagues / managers)

💡 Tip

Tell AI the audience: "Write a formal email to a client" or "Write a friendly WhatsApp to a colleague".

📋 Key Takeaways

1️⃣

AI doesn't understand your workplace purpose

Always specify the text type — memo, email, guide — don't let AI guess.

2️⃣

Tone and style must fit your audience

Formal for clients and managers, friendly for colleagues — always check.

3️⃣

Language function shapes the text

Instruction, offer, request, suggestion — each creates different text. Name the function in your prompt.

🧰 Purpose Check Checklist

Before you accept AI output, ask:
✅ Is this the right text type?
✅ Is the tone right for this audience?
✅ Does it do what I need? (instruction / offer / request / suggestion…)
