1.2 Why Does AI Make Things Up?

AI models are not search engines. They are "text generators" that guess which word comes next — like autocomplete on your phone:

Messages

I went to the

store park moon
qwertyuiop
asdfghjkl
⇧zxcvbnm⌫

Because it is only guessing, AI can make things up (hallucinate).

⚡ Key Point

AI does not have a brain — it does not know when it is making a mistake. That's why you must always check!

💡 Pattern to Remember

AI is great at general knowledge and language tasks (grammar, spelling, rephrasing).

AI is risky for specific details (names, dates, prices, links) and up-to-date facts.

📋 Verification Quick Guide

When AI gives you specific facts, always verify. Here's when to use each tool:

📖

Grammar Book

Language rules, definitions, word usage

🗺️

Google Maps / Atlas

Locations, borders, geography

🏛️

Official Website

Regulations, policies, dates, fees

📚

Course Textbook

Course-specific info, procedures

🔍

Search Engine

Cross-check any claim quickly

👨‍🏫

Ask Instructor

Expert help for tricky questions

📌 Remember

Different types of AI mistakes need different verification tools. Always pick the most reliable and official source you can find.

📋 Key Takeaways

1️⃣

AI can hallucinate

It generates convincing but false information — fake names, dates, links, and quotes.

2️⃣

AI is a text predictor, not a fact database

It guesses the next word based on patterns — "likely" does not mean "true".

3️⃣

Always verify with reliable sources

Use grammar books, official websites, maps, textbooks, search engines, and your instructors to check AI output.
